Student-Lifestyle-Academic-Performance-Analytics-using-PowerBI
Power BI dashboard project analysing how student lifestyle habits; study hours, sleep, physical activity, social interaction, and extracurricular involvement; affect academic performance and stress levels.

Project Context
Built for a university counselor use case. The goal was to provide data-driven insights to help students optimise their daily routines for better academic outcomes while maintaining a healthy lifestyle balance.


Section 1 — Decision Support Analysis 
Question 
1 How does study hours impact academic performance across different stress levels?
2 Is there a relationship between physical activity and stress levels? 
3 Do students with higher social interaction perform better or worse academically? 
4 Does extracurricular involvement help or hurt academic grades? 
5 How do sleep patterns vary by gender and how does this reflect in grades? 
6 Do physically active students sleep more or less? 
7 Do high-stress students perform lower, equal, or higher than others? 
8 Which gender shows more balanced time allocation across activities? 
9  Which variable most strongly correlates with grades? 
10 Do students with less social time compensate with better grades? 

Section 2 — What-If Case Study 
- Identified the top 3 habits of highest-scoring students
- Segmented students into 3 lifestyle clusters and benchmarked academic performance per cluster
- Modelled grade impact of shifting 1 hour from social time to study
- Modelled stress and grade impact of shifting 1 hour from sleep to physical activity
- Designed a personalised student improvement plan dashboard with KPI tracking and goal-setting

Key Findings
- Study hours showed the strongest positive correlation with grades
- High-stress students did not always underperform — sleep quality was a stronger differentiator
- Balanced-lifestyle cluster outperformed study-heavy cluster in grades, suggesting diminishing returns on study hours alone
- Reducing social time by 1 hour had a modest but positive predicted impact on grades

 Tools Used
- Power BI Desktop
- DAX (Data Analysis Expressions)
- What-If Parameters
- Data Modelling
- K-Means inspired clustering segmentation
